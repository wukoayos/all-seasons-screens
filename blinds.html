Choosing Blinds for the QLD Climate

Brisbane summers are brutal on windows. Here's how each blind type stacks up.

Heat Rejection Ranking

  1. 1 External blinds / awnings
  2. 2 Honeycomb (cellular) blinds
  3. 3 Blockout roller blinds
  4. 4 Sunscreen roller blinds
  5. 5 Shutters
  6. 6 Venetian blinds

Humidity Resistance

  • PVC / Faux woodExcellent
  • AluminiumExcellent
  • Polyester fabricGood
  • TimberPoor — not for bathrooms

Window Orientation Guide

  • North-facing: Sunscreen mesh (3–5% openness)
  • West-facing: Blockout essential — afternoon heat is intense
  • East-facing: Light filtering for morning glare
  • South-facing: Translucent or sheer is fine

Room-by-Room Picks

  • Bedroom: Blockout roller or roman for total darkness
  • Bathroom: PVC venetian or faux wood — moisture-proof
  • Living room: Dual roller (sunscreen + blockout) for flexibility
  • Home office: Sunscreen mesh to reduce glare on screens

Child Safety

Australian mandatory standards require that corded blinds have cord loops no longer than 220mm when accessible below 1,600mm from the floor.

We strongly recommend cordless options for homes with young children: motorised, spring-operated, or shutters eliminate cord hazards entirely.

We ensure all installations comply with Australian child safety regulations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do blinds cost in Brisbane?
Prices vary by type and size. Vertical blinds start from around $50 per window, roller blinds from $60, and premium roman blinds from $120. We provide a free measure and quote so you know the exact cost before committing.
What are the best blinds for Brisbane heat?
For maximum heat rejection, external blinds are unbeatable. Indoors, honeycomb (cellular) blinds offer the best insulation. Blockout rollers with reflective backing are the most popular mid-range choice. For west-facing windows, blockout is essential.
Are roller blinds or venetians better for privacy?
Blockout roller blinds provide complete privacy when closed but offer no adjustment — they're either open or shut. Venetians let you tilt the slats for light while maintaining privacy, making them better for living areas where you want both daylight and coverage.
How long do blinds last?
Quality blinds typically last 7–10 years with normal use. Aluminium venetians and PVC verticals are the most durable. Fabric blinds may need replacing sooner if exposed to direct sun. Motorised components generally carry a 5-year warranty.
Do you install motorised blinds?
Yes. We supply and install motorised roller blinds with remote control or smart-home integration. The motorised upgrade adds $200–$300 per blind on top of the standard price. Motorised blinds are also the safest option for homes with children.
