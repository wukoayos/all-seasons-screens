Screen Repairs & Rescreening Brisbane

Don't replace it — repair it. Fast, affordable screen repairs across Brisbane and surrounding suburbs.

Common Problems We Fix

If your screens are damaged, worn, or not working properly — chances are we can fix them.

Torn or Damaged Mesh

Rips, holes, and tears in fly screen mesh from wear or impact.

Bent or Broken Frames

Screen door frames that are bent, cracked, or no longer sit square.

Worn Out Rollers & Tracks

Sliding doors that stick, jump off the track, or won't glide smoothly.

Broken or Stiff Locks

Locks that jam, won't latch properly, or have worn-out mechanisms.

Sagging Security Mesh

Security mesh that's come loose, is sagging, or has weakened fixings.

Pet Damage

Dogs and cats scratching, pushing, or tearing through fly screens.

Our Repair Services

Fly Screen Mesh Replacement

We remove the old mesh and re-spline with new fibreglass or aluminium mesh. Same-day turnaround for standard screens.

Security Screen Repairs

Frame straightening, mesh re-tensioning, and fixing loose fixings. If the frame is sound, repair is much cheaper than replacement.

Lock Replacement & Upgrade

Replace worn single-point locks with triple-lock systems. Upgrade to key-alike sets so one key fits all your doors.

Roller & Track Repairs

Clean, lubricate, or replace rollers. Straighten bent tracks. Get your sliding doors gliding smoothly again.

Pet Door Installation

Retrofit pet doors into existing screen doors. Lockable options available so you stay in control.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to rescreen a fly screen door?
Most standard fly screen doors cost between $80 and $200 to rescreen, depending on size and mesh type (fibreglass or aluminium). We include removal, new mesh, and re-splining in the price. We'll give you an exact quote on-site.
Can you repair security screen mesh or does it need replacing?
It depends on the damage. If the frame is still straight and strong, we can often re-tension or patch the mesh at a fraction of the replacement cost. If the mesh is rusted through or the frame is badly bent, a full replacement may be the better option. We'll always give you an honest assessment.
How long does a screen repair take?
Most fly screen rescreens are done same-day — often within 30 minutes per screen. Security screen repairs and lock replacements typically take 1–2 hours depending on complexity. We carry common parts in the van to minimise return visits.
Do you repair screens you didn't install?
Absolutely. We repair all brands and types of screens, regardless of who installed them. If we can source the parts, we can fix it.
