Security Doors Brisbane

Heavy-duty protection for your home — hinged, sliding, and stacking options

Why Security Doors Matter

Your Front Door Is the Most Vulnerable Entry Point

According to Queensland Police Service data, around 60–70% of home break-ins occur through doors. In South East Queensland, where homes are often open to catch the breeze, an unlocked or poorly secured door is an easy target. A quality security door changes that equation entirely.

Security screen doors let you keep your doors open for ventilation and natural light while maintaining a physical barrier against intruders. They also serve as an insect screen, so you get dual functionality from a single installation.

Break-in Deterrent

Visible security screens deter opportunistic burglars — most move on to easier targets.

Ventilation & Airflow

Keep your home cool with cross-ventilation while staying protected year-round.

Insurance Benefits

AS 5039 compliant security screens can reduce your home insurance premiums. Ask your insurer.

Property Value

Quality security doors are a selling point for buyers — they add real value to your home.

Security Door Types

We supply and install all major security door configurations to suit your home layout and budget.

Hinged Security Doors

The most common choice for front and back entries. Hinged security doors swing outward (preferred) or inward, and are available in single or double configurations.

  • Triple lock systems standard
  • Single & double door options
  • Custom sizes and colours

Sliding Security Doors

Ideal for patio and glass sliding door openings — extremely common in Queensland homes. They run on a track parallel to your existing glass door.

The premium choice for wide openings and indoor-outdoor living areas. Multiple panels stack or fold neatly to one side when fully opened.

  • Perfect for entertaining areas

  • Full security when closed
  • Premium Colorbond finishes

Materials & Security Features

What goes into a quality security door — and why material choice matters in Queensland.

316 Marine Grade Stainless Steel Mesh

The gold standard for security screens in Queensland. 316 marine grade stainless steel resists corrosion from salt air and humidity — essential for homes anywhere in South East Queensland, not just on the coast. Standard 304 grade stainless steel will corrode within a few years in Brisbane's climate.

The mesh is woven from 0.8mm wire in a tight pattern that resists cutting, provides excellent visibility from inside, and allows full airflow. It is significantly stronger than aluminium grille or fibreglass mesh.

Triple Lock Systems

A single lock at handle height leaves the top and bottom of the door vulnerable to being forced open. Triple lock systems secure the door at three points — top, middle, and bottom — all operated by a single key turn or lever.

We fit triple locks as standard on all our hinged security doors. For sliding doors, multi-point locking systems provide equivalent protection along the full height of the frame.

Emergency Exit Options

Security should never trap you inside in an emergency. Safe-S-Cape style doors feature a secondary release mechanism that allows the door to be opened from inside without a key — critical for fire safety and meeting building code requirements.

We recommend emergency exit hardware for all bedroom-adjacent security doors and anywhere required by the Building Code of Australia (BCA).

Finishes & AS 5039 Compliance

All frames are powder coated in your choice of Colorbond colours to match your home — Monument, Surfmist, Woodland Grey, and Ironstone are popular choices in Brisbane. Powder coating provides a durable, UV-resistant finish that won't peel or fade.

Our security doors meet AS 5039-2008, the Australian Standard for security screen doors and windows. This standard covers dynamic impact, knife shear, jemmy, and pull tests. AS 5039 compliance is what your insurer looks for when offering premium reductions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about security doors in Brisbane.

How much does a security door cost in Brisbane?
A basic aluminium grille security door starts from around $400 installed. Mid-range stainless steel mesh doors are typically $700–$1,200, while premium options like stainless steel security screens range from $1,200–$2,000+. Sliding doors cost more due to the larger frame and track system, generally $1,000–$2,500+. The final price depends on door size, mesh material, lock type, and colour. We provide free measure and quote so you know the exact cost before committing.
What's the difference between aluminium grille and stainless steel mesh?
Aluminium grille doors use diamond-pattern aluminium bars. They are the most affordable option and provide a basic level of security, but the grille pattern is easy to see from outside and can be cut with basic tools. Stainless steel mesh doors use a tight-woven mesh that is significantly harder to cut, provides much better visibility (you can see out but people can't easily see in), and meets AS 5039 security testing standards. For genuine security, stainless steel mesh is the better choice.
Do security doors come with triple locks?
We fit triple lock systems as standard on all our hinged security doors. A triple lock secures the door at three points (top, middle, and bottom) using a single key turn. For sliding security doors, we use multi-point locking systems that provide equivalent full-height security. Triple locks are essential for meeting AS 5039 compliance and for insurance purposes.
How long does installation take?
A standard hinged security door typically takes 1–2 hours to install. Sliding doors may take 2–3 hours depending on the opening size and whether track modifications are needed. From measure to installation, the typical turnaround is 2–4 weeks, as each door is custom-made to your exact measurements. We work around your schedule and always leave the site clean.
Can I get a security screen for my sliding door?
Absolutely — sliding security doors are one of our most popular products. They are designed to fit into the existing track of your glass sliding door, running parallel on the outer side. We make them in all standard and custom sizes, with stainless steel mesh or stainless steel security screens options available. They are particularly important in Queensland homes where patio and glass sliding doors are a common entry point for break-ins.
