Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about security screens, blinds and our services

How much do security screens cost in Brisbane?
Security screen door prices in Brisbane typically range from $400 to $1,200 depending on the size, mesh type, and security rating. premium stainless steel security screens are at the premium end. We provide free measure and quote so you get an exact price for your home.
What is the difference between premium stainless steel security screens and regular security screens?
Premium stainless steel security screens use a patented mechanical clamping system that locks the mesh into the frame, making them resistant to knife attacks, jemmy bars, and extreme impacts. Regular security screens use a rubber-spline wedge system which is effective but doesn't offer the same level of forced-entry protection.
Do you offer free quotes for security screens in Brisbane?
Yes, we offer completely free measure and quote for all products — security doors, fly screens, stainless steel security screens, blinds, and rescreening. We come to your home, measure up, and provide a no-obligation quote on the spot. We service Capalaba, Cleveland, Wynnum, Redland Bay, and all of Brisbane and surrounding suburbs.
How long does it take to install security screens?
A standard security screen door installation takes about 1-2 hours. For a full house (multiple doors and windows), it usually takes a full day. After your free measure and quote, we order your custom screens — turnaround is typically 2-4 weeks for standard products and 4-6 weeks for stainless steel security screens.
What areas in Brisbane do you service?
We're based in Capalaba and service all of Brisbane and surrounding suburbs including Cleveland, Wynnum, Manly, Redland Bay, Victoria Point, Thornlands, Alexandra Hills, Birkdale, Ormiston, Wellington Point, Mount Cotton, Sheldon, and Chandler. Contact us if your suburb isn't listed — we cover a wide area.
Can you repair or rescreen my existing security door?
Yes, we offer repairs and rescreening services for existing doors and windows. This includes mesh replacement, lock repairs, roller and track fixes, and frame adjustments. Rescreening is often much cheaper than a full replacement and can extend the life of your screens by many years.
